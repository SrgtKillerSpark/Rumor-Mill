## Full Description

Rumor Mill is a medieval social strategy game about the mechanics of gossip. You play as a hired agent in a town of thirty NPCs — each with a personality, a faction, a daily schedule, and a web of relationships. Your only weapon is information.

Plant the right rumor with the right person, watch it travel through the social network, and guide it toward your objective before time runs out.

There is no combat. There are no levels. There is only the town, the people in it, and what they believe about each other.

---

## Key Features

- **30-NPC living social simulation** — every character has faction loyalties, relationship weights, and a daily routine. The network state changes in real time as rumors travel.
- **Rumor propagation engine** — stories mutate as they move. A subtle hint becomes a scandal becomes a legend. Every spreader changes the details based on their personality.
- **Social Graph Overlay** — a full-town network visualization showing every NPC, faction, and active rumor thread in real time. Watch your whisper move.
- **Rumor Crafting Panel** — choose your subject, your claim type (accusation, scandal, illness scare, prophecy), and your seeder. Each combination produces different spread patterns and mutation rates.
- **Intel system** — observe NPCs at their workplaces and eavesdrop on conversations to build an intelligence picture before committing to a move.
- **Player Journal** — track reputation scores, active rumors, and known relationships across the whole town.
- **Four handcrafted scenarios** — offense, precision targeting, dual-front campaign against an AI opponent, and a purely defensive scenario. Each demands a different strategy.
- **Day/night cycle** — NPC schedules shift through the day. Timing matters.
- **Five factions** — Merchants' Guild, Town Guard, Clergy, Common Folk, Nobility — each with their own information networks and credibility dynamics.

## Scenarios

### The Alderman's Ruin
Discredit a nine-year incumbent before the autumn tax rolls are signed. Thirty in-game days. One high-reputation target with a dense network of allies.

### The Plague Scare
A business rival has arrived in town. Spread an illness rumor precisely enough to drive her out — but the Chapel monitors the town's moral health, and one overreach triggers a counter-investigation.

### The Succession
Raise one NPC's reputation and ruin another simultaneously, while keeping your client's hands completely clean. Twenty-five days. Two targets. Zero margin for error.

### The Holy Inquisition
Purely defensive play. An inquisitor has arrived with three names on his list. 20 days to keep their reputations intact. There is no offensive option.

## Tone & Positioning

Rumor Mill is not a cozy game. It is a social strategy puzzle with a simulation underneath it. The writing is dry and morally unambiguous — you are doing something unkind to people who probably do not deserve it.

**Comparable titles:** Crusader Kings III (faction politics), Disco Elysium (text-heavy social intelligence), Cultist Simulator (systems-first opacity, dark tone), 80 Days (time pressure, information asymmetry).

**Target player:** Someone who wants to feel clever, enjoys social puzzles, and is comfortable occupying a morally grey position in a story.

---

## Development History

Rumor Mill began as a TypeScript/HTML5 Canvas prototype validating the core loop: plant, watch, adapt. The project migrated to Godot 4.6 for the full production version, enabling the isometric town view, the real-time Social Graph Overlay, and the NPC behavior simulation.

Current state: all four core scenarios are playable. The full 30-NPC simulation, rumor propagation engine, social graph visualization, and journal system are implemented. Audio is in placeholder state.
